## What is a Front End System Design Interview?

- Open-ended style questions — no single correct answer
- Candidate is given a vague problem or scenario
- Expected to ask clarifying questions and propose a suitable software design
- Can be done on a whiteboard, or in virtual interviews via a collaborative drawing app (e.g. FigJam, Excalidraw)

> 💡 Unlike coding, system design interviews test how you **think and communicate** — not just what you know.

---

## Why it's harder than it looks

- Open-ended = hard to practice for
- Many candidates have no real-life experience with large-scale systems
- Very few resources exist specifically for FE System Design
- Most existing resources target General SWEs and focus on distributed (back-end) systems

---

## System design performance = the deciding factor at senior level

> For senior and beyond, a weak system design round almost always results in a career rejection, and even a passing grade rarely makes up for a bad system design. Treat it with the same weight as coding, not as a secondary round.

---

## Key differences from Back End / Full Stack SD

See: [[FE vs BE System Design]] — full comparison table

**TL;DR:**

- BE focuses on **distributed infrastructure** — servers, databases, caches, queues
- FE focuses on **client internals** — components, state, rendering, UX, performance
- FE interviews de-emphasize back-of-the-envelope capacity math
- FE deep dives go into: [[Performance Optimization]], UX, [[Accessibility (A11y)]], [[Internationalization (i18n)]]
