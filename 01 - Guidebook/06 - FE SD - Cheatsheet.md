## RADIO Framework

|Step|What to do|
|---|---|
|**R** — Requirements exploration|Understand the problem thoroughly. Determine scope by asking clarifying questions.|
|**A** — Architecture / High-level design|Identify the key components of the product and how they relate to each other.|
|**D** — Data model|Describe the data entities, the fields they contain, and which component(s) they belong to.|
|**I** — Interface definition (API)|Define the interface (API) between components: functionality, parameters, and responses.|
|**O** — Optimizations & Deep dive|Discuss [[Performance Optimization]], [[Accessibility (A11y)]], [[Networking & APIs]], [[Rendering Strategies]], and other relevant deep-dives.|

Full breakdown: [[03 - RADIO Framework]]

## Evaluation Axes

1. **Problem exploration** — Demonstrated understanding of the problem, asked clarifying questions to reduce ambiguities and gathered sufficient requirements.
2. **Architecture** — Broke down the problem into smaller independent parts, defined their responsibilities and how these components can work together.
3. **Technical proficiency** — Demonstrated technical knowledge of front end domain fundamentals (e.g. [[Performance Optimization]], [[Networking & APIs]], [[Accessibility (A11y)]], [[Internationalization (i18n)]], security), relevant technologies and APIs.
4. **Exploration and tradeoffs** — Suggested various possible approaches, explained the pros and cons of each, and made recommendations.
5. **Product and UX sense** — Showed consideration for user experience and decisions to make the product a great one.
6. **Communication and collaboration** — Conveyed thoughts and ideas clearly and concisely, receptive to feedback, collaborated with the interviewer.

## Common Mistakes

- Jumping into answering immediately without asking questions and gathering requirements.
- Approaching the question in an unstructured manner, going all over the place and missing out on important areas.
- Insisting on only one solution or the best solution without realising that each solution has tradeoffs.
- Remaining silent the entire time and only thinking in their head.
- Going down a rabbit hole and spending too much time on unimportant areas.
- Using buzzwords without being able to explain them.
